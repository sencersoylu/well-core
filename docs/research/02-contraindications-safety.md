# HBOT Contraindications & Safety — Wellcore Health Screening Source

> **Scope.** This document is the evidence base behind Wellcore's onboarding *Health Screening* cards, the pre-session checklist, and in-app safety education. Wellcore is a **wellness companion** for people who already own or attend home hyperbaric chambers (mostly soft 1.3 ATA, some hard 1.5–2.0 ATA). It is **not** a medical device, does not control hardware, and does not deliver therapy. The goal of screening is to surface "talk to a clinician first" gates — not to clear users for treatment.
> **Date of compilation:** 2026-05-02. Primary sources: UHMS *Hyperbaric Oxygen Therapy Indications* 14th & 15th editions, FDA HBOT guidance (2021/2024), Frontiers in Medicine 2023 systematic review/meta-analysis of HBOT adverse events, UHM Journal real-world cohorts, and the Turkish Sağlık Bakanlığı *Hiperbarik Oksijen Tedavisi Uygulanan Özel Sağlık Kuruluşları Hakkında Yönetmelik* (RG 24480, 2001, with amendments).

---

## TL;DR

- **Only one absolute contraindication is universally agreed in modern hyperbaric medicine: untreated pneumothorax.** Everything else commonly listed (chemotherapy, pregnancy, claustrophobia, COPD, etc.) is *relative* — meaning a clinician should evaluate before sessions, not that HBOT is forever forbidden.
- **At 1.3 ATA on air or near-air FiO₂ (typical home soft-chamber profile), the dominant risk by an order of magnitude is middle-ear barotrauma.** Real-world incidence in clinical (higher-pressure) HBOT cohorts is ~9–10% per patient course, but ≥84% are minor (Wallace–Teed grade 0–1). CNS oxygen toxicity below 1.5 ATA is rare enough to be effectively a non-issue for users on a 1.3 ATA soft-chamber protocol; it is non-trivial above 2.0 ATA.
- **Five health-screening cards plus two soft-warning cards** cover >95% of safety-relevant cases without making the onboarding feel medical: pneumothorax/lung surgery, ENT/ear-pressure issues, recent eye surgery, pregnancy, chemotherapy/disulfiram, and (soft) seizure history, severe claustrophobia.
- **Pre-session safety is a fire-safety problem**, not just a physiology problem. 100% cotton clothing, no cosmetics/petroleum products, no electronics with lithium batteries, ear-equalization rehearsed before descent.
- **Turkey-specific:** The 2001 Yönetmelik (RG 24480) regulates *clinical* HBOT centers — not personal/home chambers. Wellcore is therefore outside the Yönetmelik's scope, but the app's medical-device exposure under the Tıbbi Cihaz Yönetmeliği (MDR-aligned) hinges entirely on whether we make therapeutic claims. **Do not.**

## Absolute contraindications

The literature consistently lists **one** absolute contraindication; some texts add a second (concurrent bleomycin) but most modern reviews demote it to "relative."

- **Untreated pneumothorax (including tension pneumothorax).** Trapped pleural air expands per Boyle's law on ascent and can cause lung collapse, mediastinal shift, or cardiovascular collapse. Must be drained (thoracostomy) before any pressurized exposure. *Source: UHMS Indications Manual 14th ed., 2019 (Moon, ed.), reaffirmed 15th ed., 2023; StatPearls "Hyperbaric Oxygen Therapy Contraindications," NBK557661.*
- **(Conditional absolute) Concurrent bleomycin within recent weeks.** Historically absolute due to risk of fulminant pulmonary fibrosis. Recent reviews (Feldmeier et al.; UHMS 15th ed.) reclassify as relative if exposure was >6 months prior and pulmonary function is normal. For Wellcore self-screening purposes we treat any reported active or recent (≤6 months) bleomycin as a hard stop.

Everything else — including doxorubicin, disulfiram, COPD, pregnancy, seizure history, claustrophobia — is **relative** in modern UHMS guidance.

## Relative contraindications (require clinician sign-off)

Grouped by system. Each entry is "what to flag" + "why" + source.

### Respiratory

- **COPD with CO₂ retention / hypercapnia.** Hyperoxia can blunt hypoxic respiratory drive and worsen hypercapnia. *UHMS 14th ed. ch. 24; StatPearls NBK557661.*
- **Bullous lung disease, large emphysematous blebs.** Same mechanism as pneumothorax — trapped air expands on ascent, risk of barotraumatic rupture. Pre-session chest imaging is recommended in clinical settings. *Heyboer et al., Int J Chron Obstruct Pulmon Dis, 2017.*
- **Recent thoracic surgery (≤3 months) or recent pneumothorax history.** Air may persist in interstitial planes. *UHMS 14th ed.*
- **Active asthma, especially poorly controlled.** Bronchospasm risk; air-trapping mimics bullae behavior. *StatPearls NBK557661.*
- **Upper respiratory infection / severe nasal congestion.** Not dangerous per se, but eustachian-tube dysfunction makes barotrauma near-certain. Defer until resolved.

### ENT

- **Recent middle-ear or sinus surgery (typically <6 weeks).** Healing tympanic membrane and graft sites are vulnerable. *Plafki et al., Aviat Space Environ Med, 2000.*
- **Eustachian tube dysfunction / inability to autoinflate.** The single biggest predictor of middle-ear barotrauma; 9-step inflation/deflation test is the clinical screen. *Karahatay et al., Ear Nose Throat J, 2008.*
- **Active sinusitis, severe rhinitis.** Sinus squeeze risk on descent.

### Cardiac

- **Uncontrolled hypertension** (typically systolic >180 / diastolic >110). Hyperoxic vasoconstriction can spike BP further. *UHMS 14th ed. ch. 25.*
- **Recent MI (<4 weeks) or unstable angina.** Defer for clinician review.
- **Decompensated congestive heart failure (EF <35%).** Hyperoxia-induced fluid shifts can precipitate pulmonary edema. *Heyboer 2017.*
- **Pacemakers / ICDs.** Modern devices are generally pressure-rated; older or non-tested devices should be verified by manufacturer letter. Not a hard stop, but a clinician check.

### Pregnancy

- **Pregnancy (any trimester) for elective wellness use.** Theoretical concerns about fetal vasoconstriction (ductus arteriosus) at therapeutic pressures; clinical experience in CO-poisoning cohorts shows safety in *emergent* indications, but elective wellness use is not justified. **Hard gate** for Wellcore. *Van Hoesen et al., JAMA, 1989; UHMS narrative review, PMC10352564, 2023.*

### Neurologic

- **Uncontrolled epilepsy / recent seizure (<3 months).** CNS oxygen toxicity manifests as seizure; pre-existing seizure threshold matters more at higher pressures. PLOS One 2025 retrospective cohort (n≈600) found HBOT broadly safe in seizure-history patients with appropriate protocol modifications. *Sanford et al., PLOS One, 2025; PMC11731757.*
- **Active CNS infection, recent stroke (<2 weeks for elective use).**

### Metabolic / Endocrine

- **Diabetes on insulin or sulfonylureas — hypoglycemia risk.** Hyperoxia improves peripheral glucose uptake; multiple case series report intra-session hypoglycemia. Recommend pre-session glucose check. *Ekanayake & Doolette, Diving Hyperb Med, 2010.*
- **Active fever ≥38 °C.** Lowers seizure threshold and amplifies oxygen toxicity risk. Defer.

### Ophthalmic

- **Recent intraocular surgery (cataract, retinal detachment, vitrectomy ≤4 weeks).** Gas bubble (SF₆, C₃F₈) used in retinal surgery expands catastrophically with pressure change — this is a *hard* gate while gas is present (4–8 weeks depending on agent). *American Society of Retina Specialists patient advisory.*
- **Progressive cataract.** Long-course HBOT (>20 sessions at ≥2.0 ATA) accelerates nuclear sclerosis; near-irrelevant at 1.3 ATA but worth flagging for hard-chamber users on long protocols. *Palmquist et al., Br J Ophthalmol, 1984; Frontiers Med 2023 meta-analysis.*

### Psychiatric

- **Severe claustrophobia, panic disorder.** Not physiologically dangerous but a high abort-rate predictor; warrants discussion before purchase, not a hard medical block.

### Medications

- **Doxorubicin** — synergistic cardiotoxicity; allow ≥72 h washout, longer if cumulative dose is high. *StatPearls NBK560873.*
- **Bleomycin** — pulmonary fibrosis risk (see absolute section).
- **Cisplatin** — impairs wound healing; *relative*, not a safety hazard for wellness use.
- **Disulfiram (Antabuse)** — inhibits superoxide dismutase, increases oxygen-toxicity seizure risk. *Faiman et al., Clin Pharmacol Ther, 1980.*
- **Mafenide acetate (Sulfamylon)** — relevant for burn patients only; topical CA inhibitor causes CO₂ retention.

### Smoking / nicotine

Not a contraindication per se, but nicotine causes peripheral vasoconstriction that partly negates HBOT's perfusion benefit. UHMS chapter on diabetic foot ulcer recommends ≥2 hours pre-session abstinence for therapeutic benefit; this is a *coaching* item, not a screening gate.

## Recommended Health Screening cards (5 hard + 2 soft)

The onboarding renders these as yes/no cards. Any "yes" on a **hard** card surfaces a red-flag state ("Talk to a clinician before your first session — here's why") and disables the *Start First Session* CTA until the user toggles a "I've spoken to a clinician" confirmation. Any "yes" on a **soft** card shows an inline coaching tip without blocking.

### Card 1 — Lung & chest *(HARD)*

- **EN:** *Have you ever been told you have a collapsed lung (pneumothorax), severe COPD/emphysema, lung blebs or bullae, or had chest/lung surgery in the last 3 months?*
- **TR:** *Size hiç akciğer sönmesi (pnömotoraks), ileri KOAH/amfizem, akciğerde bül/kist tanısı kondu mu ya da son 3 ay içinde göğüs/akciğer ameliyatı geçirdiniz mi?*
- **Triggers:** Red flag.
- **Source:** UHMS 14th ed.; StatPearls NBK557661. The only *absolute* contraindication lives here.

### Card 2 — Ears, sinuses & recent ENT surgery *(HARD)*

- **EN:** *Right now, do you have a cold, sinus infection, severely blocked nose, or have you had ear or sinus surgery in the last 6 weeks?*
- **TR:** *Şu anda nezle, sinüzit, ileri burun tıkanıklığınız var mı veya son 6 hafta içinde kulak ya da sinüs ameliyatı geçirdiniz mi?*
- **Triggers:** Red flag.
- **Source:** Karahatay et al. 2008; Plafki et al. 2000. ETD is the #1 driver of barotrauma.

### Card 3 — Recent eye surgery *(HARD)*

- **EN:** *Have you had any eye surgery in the last 6 weeks, especially retinal surgery with a "gas bubble"?*
- **TR:** *Son 6 hafta içinde göz ameliyatı geçirdiniz mi — özellikle "gaz baloncuğu" konulan retina ameliyatı?*
- **Triggers:** Red flag.
- **Source:** ASRS patient advisory; intraocular gas + pressure change is acutely dangerous.

### Card 4 — Pregnancy *(HARD)*

- **EN:** *Are you currently pregnant or trying to become pregnant?*
- **TR:** *Şu anda hamile misiniz veya hamile kalmaya mı çalışıyorsunuz?*
- **Triggers:** Red flag (hard gate while pregnant; soft for "trying").
- **Source:** UHMS narrative review, PMC10352564.

### Card 5 — Cancer treatment & specific medications *(HARD)*

- **EN:** *Are you currently receiving — or did you finish in the last 6 months — chemotherapy (especially bleomycin, doxorubicin, or cisplatin), or are you taking disulfiram (Antabuse)?*
- **TR:** *Son 6 ay içinde kemoterapi (özellikle bleomisin, doksorubisin, sisplatin) aldınız ya da almakta mısınız? Disulfiram (Antabus) kullanıyor musunuz?*
- **Triggers:** Red flag.
- **Source:** StatPearls NBK560873; Faiman 1980.

### Card 6 — Seizure / epilepsy history *(SOFT)*

- **EN:** *Do you have a history of seizures or epilepsy, or take medication for one?*
- **TR:** *Geçmişte nöbet ya da epilepsi öyküsü var mı, ya da bunun için ilaç kullanıyor musunuz?*
- **Triggers:** Soft warning + recommend clinician chat for hard-chamber (≥1.5 ATA) users; near-irrelevant at 1.3 ATA on air.
- **Source:** Sanford et al., PLOS One 2025; Welslau & Almeling, UHM Journal, 2004 (n=80,000 sessions, seizure incidence ≈1:10,000 below 2 ATA).

### Card 7 — Heart, blood pressure & diabetes *(SOFT)*

- **EN:** *Do you have uncontrolled high blood pressure, heart failure, an implanted pacemaker/defibrillator, or insulin-treated diabetes?*
- **TR:** *Kontrolsüz yüksek tansiyon, kalp yetmezliği, kalp pili/ICD veya insülin kullandığınız diyabet var mı?*
- **Triggers:** Soft warning + clinician-chat tip + pacemaker pressure-rating reminder.
- **Source:** UHMS 14th ed. ch. 25; Heyboer 2017.

> *Implementation note:* All seven cards together fit on one scrollable screen. The "yes" rate in published clinical screening is ~15–25%; expect onboarding drop-off if cards 6–7 are made hard gates. Keep them soft.

## Pre-session safety checklist

Render as a 6-bullet checklist on the *Pre-Session* screen. User must tap-confirm each item before the *Start session timer* button enables.

- **Hydrate** — drink ~250 ml water in the hour before; oxygen + dryness causes mild dehydration headache.
- **Empty bladder/bowels** — there's no exit mid-session for a 60–90 min run.
- **No cosmetics, lotions, perfume, hair products, deodorant, or lip balm.** Petroleum-based products are fuel in oxygen-enriched air. *FDA Letter to Health Care Providers, "Follow Instructions for Safe Use of Hyperbaric Oxygen Therapy Devices," 2021/updated 2024; UIHC fire-safety guidance.*
- **Wear 100% cotton, loose-fitting clothing.** No synthetics, no metal zippers/underwire, no static-prone fabrics. *NFPA 99 Ch. 14; UIHC.*
- **No phones, smartwatches, e-cigs, vape pens, hand warmers, or anything with a lithium battery inside the chamber** unless your chamber's manufacturer explicitly approves it. (Wellcore runs the timer on a phone *outside* the chamber by design.)
- **Rehearse equalization** — perform a gentle Valsalva or Toynbee on the surface before sealing the chamber. If your ears don't pop easily right now, abort and reschedule.

**During session:** if you feel ear pain that does not clear, sinus pain, chest pain, vision change, sudden anxiety, twitching/lip-numbness, or shortness of breath — *signal the operator and abort the session*. For solo home users, this means hitting the manufacturer-specified emergency vent.

## Ear equalization (in-app education content)

Ear pain on descent is the single most common adverse event. Equalize **early and often** — every 30–60 seconds during the first 3–5 minutes of pressurization, *before* you feel pressure. Once it hurts, it's already too late and the eustachian tube may have collapsed.

### Valsalva — easiest, most common

Pinch your nose closed, keep your mouth shut, and gently push air into your nose as if blowing it. You should feel a soft "pop" in each ear. **Gentle.** Do not strain — too much pressure can rupture the round window. If one ear pops and the other doesn't, tilt your head toward the unpopped side and try again.

### Toynbee — when Valsalva is too forceful

Pinch your nose closed and **swallow** with your mouth shut. The swallow plus closed nose mechanically opens the eustachian tubes. Some people find this works when Valsalva doesn't, especially if there's mild congestion.

### Frenzel — gentlest, hardest to learn

Pinch your nose, close your throat (as if starting to lift something heavy), and make a hard "**K**" sound with the back of your tongue. The tongue acts like a piston, pushing air up through the eustachian tubes without raising chest pressure. Safer for people with cardiovascular issues; takes practice.

### Abort criteria (in-app red text)

- If after **two attempts** at any technique you cannot equalize, **stop the descent.** Most home soft chambers can be partially vented to back off ~0.05 ATA — do that, equalize, then resume.
- If you feel sharp pain, hear a "squeak" or sudden hearing loss, or feel vertigo — **end the session entirely** and see an ENT before the next one.
- Never force a Valsalva. Ruptured eardrums and round-window fistulas are rare but real.

## Adverse event rates (mild HBOT vs. standard HBOT)

| Event | Mild HBOT (≤1.5 ATA, soft chamber, near-air FiO₂) | Standard HBOT (2.0–2.8 ATA, 100% O₂) | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Middle-ear barotrauma** (any grade, per patient course) | ~3–6% (estimated, lower than clinical due to slower compression) | 9.2–10% per course; ~0.6% per session | Lehm & Bennett, UHM 2019 (n=5,962); Camporesi UHM 2020 (n=2,610) |
| **Severe MEB requiring myringotomy** | <0.1% | ~1–2% of MEB cases (≤0.2% overall) | Lehm 2019 |
| **Sinus barotrauma** | ~1% | ~2% | Frontiers Med systematic review, 2023 (PMC10232961) |
| **CNS oxygen-toxicity seizure** | Effectively negligible at 1.3 ATA on air; <1:100,000 sessions | ~1:10,000 sessions ≤2 ATA; ~1:2,000 ≥2.4 ATA | Welslau UHM 2004 (n=80,000); Sanford PLOS One 2025 |
| **Pulmonary oxygen toxicity** | Not observed | Cumulative >300 UPTD/day | Clark & Lambertsen, 1971 |
| **Reversible myopia** | Not reported below 2.0 ATA | ~20% of patients on >20-session courses | Frontiers Med 2023 |
| **Cataract progression** | Not reported below 2.0 ATA | Reported on courses >150 sessions | Palmquist 1984 |
| **Claustrophobic abort** | ~2–4% | ~2–4% | Heyboer 2017 |
| **Fire incidents** | Extraordinarily rare; near-zero in soft chambers operating at <1.4 ATA on air | Historical clinical incidents linked to ignition sources + 100% O₂ | NFPA 99; FDA HCP letter 2021 |

> **Bottom line for 1.3 ATA soft-chamber users:** the only adverse event Wellcore should design around is middle-ear barotrauma. Everything else is either negligible at this pressure or a hard-chamber clinical concern.

## Disclaimer language

Rendered on **first launch (one-time, accept-to-continue)** and again — shorter — on the **pre-first-session** screen.

### First-launch (long form)

> **Wellcore is a wellness companion, not a medical device.** It does not diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease. Wellcore does not control any hyperbaric chamber and does not deliver oxygen. The information, timers, journals, and recommendations in this app are educational and lifestyle-focused.
>
> **Hyperbaric oxygen therapy is FDA-cleared for 13 specific medical conditions** — including decompression sickness, carbon monoxide poisoning, certain non-healing wounds, and radiation injury — when delivered in an accredited clinical setting. Use of personal hyperbaric chambers (especially soft chambers at ≤1.4 ATA) for any condition other than acute mountain sickness is considered **off-label**. The U.S. FDA, the Undersea & Hyperbaric Medical Society, and equivalent regulators in the EU and Turkey have not approved soft-chamber HBOT as a treatment for cancer, autism, Alzheimer's, long COVID, autoimmune conditions, or general anti-aging. Wellcore makes no such claims.
>
> **You are responsible for your own safety.** If you have any medical condition — including but not limited to lung, ear, sinus, eye, heart, or seizure conditions; pregnancy; or recent surgery or chemotherapy — talk to a qualified clinician *before* using a hyperbaric chamber. Follow your chamber manufacturer's instructions exactly. By tapping "I understand," you confirm you have read this notice and accept that any use of a hyperbaric chamber is at your own risk and judgment.

### Pre-first-session (short form)

> **Quick reminder before your first session.** Wellcore does not control your chamber and does not replace medical advice. If anything during the session feels wrong — ear pain that won't clear, chest pain, vision changes, lip numbness, twitching, sudden anxiety — **end the session and seek medical attention**. By tapping "Start," you confirm your chamber and operating environment meet the manufacturer's safety requirements.

## Turkish Ministry of Health regulatory note

- The governing regulation is the **"Hiperbarik Oksijen Tedavisi Uygulanan Özel Sağlık Kuruluşları Hakkında Yönetmelik"** (Resmî Gazete sayı 24480, 01.08.2001, with subsequent amendments). It regulates **clinical** HBOT centers — staffing, chamber specifications, physician supervision, accreditation, inspection — and does **not** address personal/home chambers or wellness applications. Wellcore is therefore outside its scope.
- The relevant medical-device law is the **Tıbbi Cihaz Yönetmeliği** (transposing EU MDR 2017/745). A consumer app becomes a regulated medical device the moment it makes a **medical claim** — diagnosis, treatment, prevention, monitoring of disease. The Wellcore positioning ("wellness companion, journaling, education, timer") sits cleanly in the *lifestyle/wellness* zone provided we **never** claim Wellcore (or HBOT) treats a specific disease. This is the single most important compliance line to hold in copy and marketing.
- **TİTCK (Türkiye İlaç ve Tıbbi Cihaz Kurumu)** is the relevant authority. If Wellcore later adds features that could be construed as medical (symptom-tracking with diagnostic suggestions, dose recommendations, integration with chamber control), a Class I or higher CE/UDI registration becomes likely.
- **Practical guardrails for Sencer:** (a) onboarding screening cards must read "talk to a clinician before sessions," never "Wellcore approves you to use HBOT"; (b) educational content on conditions (autism, long COVID, etc.) must be framed as "what the literature says" with citations, never as "Wellcore recommends HBOT for X"; (c) the disclaimer above should be available in Turkish and shown as part of KVKK-compliant onboarding consent.
