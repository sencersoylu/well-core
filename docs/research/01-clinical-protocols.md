# HBOT Clinical Protocols — Evidence Map for Wellcore

_Last updated: 2026-05-02. Scope: peer-reviewed literature 2018–2026, weighted toward RCTs and systematic reviews. Wellness-app educational use only — not medical advice._

## TL;DR

- The strongest modern HBOT evidence outside its UHMS-approved clinical indications comes from a single Israeli research group (Efrati, Hadanny, Hachmo, Zilberman-Itskovich at Shamir Medical Center / Aviv) using a **2.0 ATA, 60–90 min, 5×/week, 40–60 sessions** protocol — not the 1.3 ATA "mild HBOT" most home users run.
- Replicated, sham-controlled RCT-grade signal exists for: **long COVID cognition** (Zilberman-Itskovich 2022), **healthy-aging cognition** (Hadanny 2020), **fibromyalgia pain** (Efrati 2015, 2024), and **skin aging biomarkers** (Hachmo 2021). All used hard-chamber 2.0 ATA.
- For **athletic recovery**, meta-analyses are mixed: HBOT does not reliably boost performance pre/post-exercise but can accelerate recovery from exercise-induced muscle damage.
- For **anti-aging / radiance / vitality / wellness**, evidence is mostly mechanistic + single-site clinical trials; no large multi-center confirmatory RCTs exist as of May 2026.
- **Mild HBOT at 1.3 ATA** (the typical home soft-chamber dose) has substantially weaker direct evidence; most positive trials in cognition, skin, and pain use ≥1.5 ATA. App copy must reflect this honestly.

## Mechanism overview (1 paragraph for app onboarding)

HBOT exposes the body to oxygen at higher-than-atmospheric pressure, dissolving roughly 10–15× more oxygen into plasma than breathing room air at sea level. Researchers describe a "hyperoxic-hypoxic paradox": the repeated cycling between high-oxygen sessions and the relative drop afterward stabilizes Hypoxia-Inducible Factor 1-alpha (HIF-1α) and related transcription factors as if the tissue were oxygen-starved, even though it is oxygen-rich. Studies report this triggers downstream effects including new blood vessel growth (angiogenesis via VEGF), mobilization of stem and progenitor cells from bone marrow, anti-inflammatory cytokine shifts, improved mitochondrial function, and — in the Hachmo trial — telomere lengthening and reduction of senescent immune cells. These mechanisms are the proposed biological basis for the cognitive, skin, and pain findings described below; they are well-established in animal and cellular models and increasingly supported by human imaging and biomarker data.

## Per-goal evidence

### radiance (skin, anti-aging cosmetic)
- **Pressure / duration / frequency:** 2.0 ATA, 90 min, 5 days/week, 60 sessions (Hachmo 2021 protocol).
- **Onset timeline:** Skin biopsy changes (collagen density, elastic fiber length, vessel count up; fragmentation and senescent cell counts down) measured after **3 months / 60 sessions**. No published earlier timepoints.
- **Key studies:** Hachmo Y. et al., 2021, _Aging (Albany NY)_, n=33 healthy adults ≥64 yrs, prospective trial. Significant increase in collagen density and elastic fiber length, decrease in senescent fibroblasts. (PMID: 34784294.)
- **Evidence strength:** Moderate (single-site, no sham control on skin endpoint; histological + objective).
- **Defensible app copy:** "In a 2021 trial, healthy older adults completing a 60-session HBOT protocol showed measurable improvements in skin collagen density and elastic-fiber length on biopsy."
- **Disclaimer flag:** Yes — must clarify protocol used hard-chamber 2.0 ATA, not soft-chamber 1.3 ATA.

### recovery (post-exercise muscle / DOMS)
- **Pressure / duration / frequency:** 2.0–2.4 ATA, 60–100 min sessions, applied within 24–48h post-exertion. Number of sessions varies (1–10).
- **Onset timeline:** Reduction of soreness and CK markers reported within **1–3 sessions** in DOMS protocols.
- **Key studies:** Yang S. et al., 2025, _J Sci Med Sport_, systematic review/meta-analysis on exercise-induced muscle injury — HBOT significantly accelerated recovery; both ≤2.0 and >2.0 ATA effective; 60-min and 100-min durations effective. (PMID: 40784513.) Marmolejo Marmolejo V. et al., 2021, _Front Physiol_ meta-analysis: pre/post-exercise HBOT did not significantly improve performance; intra-exercise HBOT may improve muscle endurance. (PMC8650584.)
- **Evidence strength:** Moderate for DOMS/muscle recovery; weak for performance gain.
- **Defensible app copy:** "Meta-analyses suggest HBOT used after intense exercise can shorten recovery from muscle soreness and damage."
- **Disclaimer flag:** No, if framed as recovery only (not performance enhancement claims).

### vitality (energy, fatigue, general)
- **Pressure / duration / frequency:** Most positive fatigue findings derive from long COVID and fibromyalgia trials at 2.0 ATA × 40–60 sessions, 5×/week.
- **Onset timeline:** Self-reported fatigue and sleep improvements at study endpoint (~8–12 weeks).
- **Key studies:** Zilberman-Itskovich S. et al., 2022, _Sci Rep_, n=73 long-COVID RCT — significant improvement in fatigue, sleep, pain, psychiatric symptoms (PMID: 35821512). Efrati S. et al., 2015, _PLoS ONE_, fibromyalgia RCT, n=60 — fatigue subdomain improved.
- **Evidence strength:** Weak as a stand-alone "vitality" claim — extrapolated from disease-state trials, no RCT in healthy fatigued adults.
- **Defensible app copy:** "Participants in long-COVID and fibromyalgia trials reported improvements in fatigue and sleep after HBOT protocols."
- **Disclaimer flag:** Yes — avoid implying HBOT cures generic tiredness.

### wellness (general healthspan)
- **Pressure / duration / frequency:** 2.0 ATA, 90 min, 5×/week, 60 sessions (Hachmo telomere protocol).
- **Onset timeline:** Telomere length increases (~20–38% in immune-cell subsets) and senescent T-cell decreases (~10–37%) measured after **60 sessions / 3 months**.
- **Key studies:** Hachmo Y. et al., 2020, _Aging (Albany NY)_, n=35 healthy adults — telomere lengthening and immunosenescence decrease (PMID: 33206062). Frontiers in Aging review (Marcinkowska et al., 2024, _Front Aging_) summarizing healthspan evidence.
- **Evidence strength:** Weak for hard healthspan endpoints (no mortality or longevity outcome data); moderate for surrogate aging biomarkers.
- **Defensible app copy:** "Studies in healthy older adults have measured changes in cellular-aging biomarkers such as telomere length after a 60-session HBOT protocol."
- **Disclaimer flag:** Yes — biomarker change is not equivalent to lifespan extension.

### brain_fog
- **Pressure / duration / frequency:** 2.0 ATA, 90 min, 5×/week, 40–60 sessions.
- **Onset timeline:** Cognitive testing improvements (attention, processing speed, executive function) measured after **3 months / 60 sessions**.
- **Key studies:** Hadanny A. et al., 2020, _Aging_, RCT n=63 healthy adults >64 — significant improvement in attention, processing speed, executive function vs. control; cerebral blood flow increases on perfusion MRI (PMID: 32589613). Zilberman-Itskovich S. et al., 2022 (long COVID, see below) showed similar cognitive benefit.
- **Evidence strength:** Moderate for older adults / post-illness cognition; absent for "brain fog" as an independent label in young healthy users.
- **Defensible app copy:** "In a randomized trial, older adults completing 60 HBOT sessions showed measurable gains in attention and information-processing speed."
- **Disclaimer flag:** Yes — extrapolating to non-clinical "brain fog" is not RCT-supported.

### long_covid
- **Pressure / duration / frequency:** 2.0 ATA, 90 min, 5×/week, **40 sessions** (Zilberman-Itskovich protocol).
- **Onset timeline:** Cognitive, fatigue, sleep, and psychiatric improvements after 40 sessions (~8 weeks); long-term follow-up at 1+ year shows benefits persist.
- **Key studies:** Zilberman-Itskovich S. et al., 2022, _Scientific Reports_ 12:11252, RCT n=73 (37 HBOT vs 36 sham) — significant group-by-time effects in global cognition (d=0.495, p=0.038), attention (d=0.477), executive function (d=0.463); MRI-DTI showed microstructural and perfusion changes (PMID: 35821512). Hadanny A. et al., 2024, _Sci Rep_ longitudinal follow-up confirming durability (PMID: 38360929). Kjellberg A. et al., 2025, _Lancet Reg Health Eur_ / related — additional placebo-controlled RCT replication.
- **Evidence strength:** Strongest of any Wellcore goal — sham-controlled RCT + replication + imaging.
- **Defensible app copy:** "A 2022 randomized controlled trial reported cognitive and quality-of-life improvements in long-COVID patients completing 40 HBOT sessions, with benefits persisting at 1-year follow-up."
- **Disclaimer flag:** Yes — long COVID is a medical condition; app must direct users to clinicians and not position itself as treatment.

### neuro_recovery (post-stroke, post-TBI, post-concussion)
- **Pressure / duration / frequency:** 1.5–2.0 ATA, 60–90 min, 40–60 sessions, 5×/week.
- **Onset timeline:** Cognitive and motor improvements measured after 40–60 sessions; persistent post-concussion symptom (PPCS) trials show effect sizes after 40 sessions at 1.5 ATA.
- **Key studies:** Hadanny A. et al., 2020, _Restor Neurol Neurosci_, retrospective cohort of late-chronic post-stroke (n>150) — significant gains across all cognitive domains after 40–60 sessions at 2 ATA (PMID: 32083593). Harch P.G. et al. and Mozayeni / Wright sub-analyses, 2022, _Front Neurol_ "Systematic Review and Dosage Analysis: HBOT in mild TBI / PPCS" — four RCTs at 1.5 ATA × 40 sessions met Level 1 evidence per Centre for Evidence-Based Medicine criteria (PMID: 35370898). Boussi-Gross R. et al., 2013, _PLoS ONE_ TBI RCT (foundational).
- **Evidence strength:** Moderate-to-strong for chronic-stage post-stroke and PPCS; weak for acute stroke (different mechanism).
- **Defensible app copy:** "Multiple RCTs report cognitive and functional gains in adults with persistent post-concussion symptoms after a 40-session HBOT protocol at 1.5 ATA."
- **Disclaimer flag:** Yes — clinical condition, requires medical oversight.

### athletic_performance
- **Pressure / duration / frequency:** 1.5–2.5 ATA, 60–90 min sessions; protocols vary widely (single-session pre-event, multi-week between-training).
- **Onset timeline:** VO2max and anaerobic-threshold gains in one trial after multi-week protocols; recovery effects sooner.
- **Key studies:** Mukherjee A. et al., 2021, _Sports Medicine - Open_, blinded RCT, middle-aged amateur athletes — HBOT increased VO2max and oxygen consumption at anaerobic threshold (PMC8005412). Branco B. et al., 2022, _Front Physiol_, "Does HBO Improve Athletic Performance?" — narrative review concluding mixed/insufficient evidence (PMID: 35900773). Sanya M. et al., 2024, _Front Physiol_ — youth football post-match recovery RCT, faster recovery markers.
- **Evidence strength:** Weak overall; better for recovery than for direct performance.
- **Defensible app copy:** "Some studies report VO2max improvements in athletes following multi-week HBOT protocols, though evidence for direct performance enhancement remains mixed."
- **Disclaimer flag:** No, but avoid "ergogenic aid" framing; HBOT is permitted by WADA but is not an established performance enhancer.

### anti_aging
- **Pressure / duration / frequency:** 2.0 ATA, 90 min, 5×/week, 60 sessions.
- **Onset timeline:** Telomere and senescence biomarker changes after 60 sessions (~3 months); skin histology likewise.
- **Key studies:** Hachmo 2020 (telomeres) and 2021 (skin), as cited above. Frontiers in Aging review by Lucas et al., 2024, _Front Aging_ "HBOT: future prospects in regenerative therapy and anti-aging" (DOI: 10.3389/fragi.2024.1368982).
- **Evidence strength:** Weak — almost entirely surrogate biomarkers from a single research group; no large RCT, no hard outcomes.
- **Defensible app copy:** "Early human studies have measured changes in cellular-aging biomarkers such as telomere length and senescent-cell counts following HBOT, though long-term effects on aging are not yet established."
- **Disclaimer flag:** Yes — strongest disclaimer warranted; do not imply life extension.

## Mild HBOT (1.3 ATA) caveats — what the home-chamber audience can and cannot expect

Most home soft chambers operate at 1.3 ATA with ambient air (effective FiO2 ~28–30% with concentrator). This is roughly a 30% increase over sea-level pressure — enough to dissolve modestly more oxygen in plasma but **substantially less than the 2.0 ATA / 100% O2 protocol used in nearly every positive RCT cited above**.

Specifically:

- **Cognition / neuro-recovery:** The best mTBI / PPCS evidence (Harch, Wright, Mozayeni reviews) used **1.5 ATA**, not 1.3. A 2009 multicenter RCT (Granpeesheh; pediatric autism) using 1.3 ATA / 24% O2 found improvement on parent and physician CGI scores, but follow-up open-label and behavior-analytic studies failed to replicate, and the UHMS Position Paper (2009, reaffirmed) does not support HBOT for autism.
- **Skin / telomere / fibromyalgia / long-COVID:** All key trials used **2.0 ATA**. There is no published RCT showing equivalent endpoints at 1.3 ATA.
- **What 1.3 ATA can plausibly offer:** Increased plasma O2 (~50% higher tissue oxygenation per OxyNova / mechanistic data), mild HIF-1α activation, subjective relaxation and sleep effects, possible recovery benefit after exertion. These are physiologically real but **not the same dose as the cited RCTs**.
- **Insurance/regulatory bodies (UnitedHealthcare, BlueCross/BlueShield SC) classify mHBOT as "unproven and not medically necessary"** for any indication, while UHMS reserves its 14–15 approved indications for ≥2.0 ATA hard-chamber protocols.

App-copy implication: When a user runs sessions at 1.3 ATA, the Wellcore content should phrase outcomes as "users in studies at higher pressures experienced X" and never imply equivalence with the published trial doses. Where a goal's evidence is entirely from 2.0 ATA studies (radiance, anti_aging, long_covid, brain_fog, wellness), the app should display a "protocol mismatch" educational note.
