# HBOT for PTSD, TBI, and Anxiety — Wellcore Evidence + Product Brief

> Research date: 2026-05-02. Author: Wellcore evidence team. All claims below are wellness-positioned; Wellcore is **not** a medical device. This brief informs goal taxonomy and onboarding copy decisions only.

---

## TL;DR

- **TBI/PPCS:** Evidence is genuinely contested. VA/DoD RCTs (Wolf 2012, Miller 2015, Cifu/HOPPS 2014, BIMA/Weaver 2018) found HBOT no better than 1.2–1.3 ATA "sham" — but the sham itself produced clinically meaningful improvements, suggesting the comparator was an active low dose. The 2025 Lindell *Sci Rep* double-blind RCT (40 TBI participants) is the first methodologically clean trial showing a 7-point NSI advantage for HBOT over true sham, durable to 12 months. **VA/DoD officially recommends *against* HBOT for mTBI** (2021 CPG; reaffirmed in July 2025 DHA Information Paper).
- **PTSD:** Two positive RCTs in treatment-resistant veterans (Doenyas-Barak/Efrati 2022 *PLoS One*; Doenyas-Barak 2024 *J Clin Psychiatry*) show large CAPS-5 reductions (~17–18 points) at 2.0 ATA × 60 sessions. Effect appears dose-dependent (≥35 % CAPS-5 drop predicts durability). Positioned as adjunct, not standalone therapy.
- **Anxiety as standalone:** No direct RCT evidence with anxiety disorders (GAD, panic, social) as primary outcome. Anxiety appears only as secondary outcome in TBI/PTSD/long-COVID trials. Mechanism (parasympathetic shift, reduced amygdala reactivity) is plausible but speculative.
- **Recommendation:** **Do NOT add PTSD, TBI, or Anxiety as standalone Wellcore goals.** Fold supportive language into existing goals (`neuro_recovery` for TBI/PPCS; `wellness` for stress/mood/anxiety-adjacent users). PTSD should be neither a goal nor a marketed-toward population — too high regulatory risk, DSM-5 diagnosis, and Doenyas-Barak protocols (2.0 ATA × 60 sessions) are clinical-grade, not home-chamber compatible.
- **Add a suicidality screening item** to onboarding for all users selecting `neuro_recovery`, `brain_fog`, `long_covid`, or `wellness` goals — gate use on negative screen, route positives to professional resources. This is independent of regulatory positioning; it is duty-of-care.
- **Pressure dependency:** Positive PTSD/TBI results cluster at **1.5–2.0 ATA**. There is **no credible evidence** that 1.3 ATA mild-chamber dosing meaningfully addresses TBI or PTSD symptoms beyond placebo. This must be reflected in expectation-setting copy for Wellcore's home-chamber audience.

## TBI (incl. mTBI / PPCS)

### RCT landscape

The HBOT-for-TBI evidence base is dominated by four DoD/VA-funded military RCTs and a handful of civilian trials:

- **Wolf et al. 2012** (*Mil Med*) — 50 active-duty mTBI service members, 1.5 ATA HBOT vs. 1.3 ATA "sham" (room air at 1.3 ATA). No between-group difference; both groups improved on PCL-M and ImPACT.
- **Miller et al. 2015 / HOPPS** (*JAMA Intern Med*) — 72 service members, 1.5 ATA O₂ vs. 1.2 ATA air vs. no-chamber. Neither chamber arm beat no-chamber on Rivermead. PMID 25506930.
- **Cifu et al. 2014** (*J Head Trauma Rehabil*) — 60 marines, multi-arm pressure-titration. No dose-response; all chamber arms equivalent.
- **BIMA / Weaver 2018** (*Undersea Hyperb Med*) — 71 service members, 1.5 ATA vs. 1.2 ATA sham. HBOT showed transient RPQ-3 advantage that **regressed at 6 and 12 months**. Adverse-event focused; not powered for efficacy.

The famous controversy: when "sham" is 1.2–1.3 ATA pressurized air, the comparator delivers ~30 % more dissolved O₂ than sea-level air and produces clinical improvement on its own — so a null result does not cleanly mean "HBOT does not work," it means "1.5 ATA O₂ does not beat 1.3 ATA air at this dose." Harch and others have argued this for a decade (Harch 2013 *Med Gas Res*).

**Positive trials.** Boussi-Gross / Hadanny / Efrati's Israeli civilian work (1.5 ATA, 40–60 sessions, crossover designs) consistently shows cognitive and quality-of-life gains in chronic mTBI 1–6 years post-injury. Hadanny et al. 2022 (*Sci Rep*) extended this to pediatric PPCS. Harch's 2017 case series (*Med Gas Res*; n = 30 service members) showed −16.6 PCL-M and SPECT perfusion gains, but it is uncontrolled.

**The 2025 inflection point.** Lindell et al. (*Scientific Reports*, Feb 2025; doi:10.1038/s41598-025-86631-6) ran a true double-blind RCT of 49 adults with persistent post-brain-injury symptoms (40 TBI). 1.5 ATA O₂ × 40 sessions vs. true sham. **Mean NSI improvement HBOT 10.6 vs. sham 3.6, between-group difference 7.0 (95 % CI 1.7–12.3, p = 0.01).** Anxiety, sleep, and vestibular gains persisted to 12 months. This is the first methodologically tight positive TBI RCT.

**Latest meta-analyses 2023–2026.** The 2024 Frontiers dosage-analysis (Harch & Andrews, doi:10.3389/fneur.2024.1360311) argues HBOT efficacy is dose-dependent and that pooling across heterogeneous protocols obscures real signal. The 2022 Hadanny systematic review (doi:10.3389/fneur.2022.815056) concluded ≥40 sessions at 1.5 ATA is the threshold. VA ESP 2018/2022 (NBK499535, NBK572533) reached the **opposite** conclusion: pooled evidence does not support short-term efficacy and any improvements are non-durable. Both readings are defensible from the same data; the divergence is dose-stratification philosophy.

### VA/DoD position

- **2021 VA/DoD CPG for Post-Acute mTBI** (healthquality.va.gov/guidelines/Rehab/mtbi/): explicit recommendation **against** HBOT for mTBI symptoms.
- **2018 ASD(HA) memorandum:** HBOT should not be prescribed for persistent post-concussion symptoms.
- **July 2025 DHA TBI Center of Excellence Information Paper** (health.mil): reaffirms 2018 stance, notes four trials consistent with no benefit over sham.
- **Counter-current:** S.2737 (119th Congress, 2025) would mandate a VA HBOT pilot program for veterans with TBI/PTSD — political pressure exists but has not changed clinical guidance.

### Pressure dependency

All positive signals — Boussi-Gross, Hadanny, Harch, Lindell 2025, Doenyas-Barak — use **1.5–2.0 ATA at 100 % O₂ for 40–60 sessions**. There is **no published RCT** demonstrating efficacy of 1.3 ATA mild-chamber protocols for TBI symptoms. Wellcore's audience is largely home-chamber (1.3–1.5 ATA range); honesty requires that any TBI-adjacent copy state the evidence base is built on clinical-grade pressures.

### Defensible app phrasing

- **Avoid:** "treats TBI", "for concussion recovery", "post-concussion syndrome".
- **Use:** "supports cognitive wellbeing in users recovering from head impacts" — and only as a sub-bullet under `neuro_recovery`, never as a standalone goal.

## PTSD

### RCT landscape

- **Harch et al. 2017** (*Med Gas Res*) — case series, 30 service members, 1.5 ATA × 40, mean PCL-M drop −16.6 (p < 0.001). SPECT perfusion gains. Uncontrolled.
- **Doenyas-Barak / Hadanny / Efrati 2022** (*PLoS One*, doi:10.1371/journal.pone.0264161; PMID 35192645). 35 IDF combat veterans with treatment-resistant PTSD. **2.0 ATA × 60 sessions × 90 min** vs. waitlist (no sham). CAPS-5 −18.1 in HBOT vs. +2.0 control. DTI showed white-matter microstructure changes in insula and amygdala. Half of HBOT participants no longer met PTSD criteria.
- **Doenyas-Barak et al. 2024** (*J Clin Psychiatry*, online Oct 2024; PMID 39566051). 63 randomized, 56 completed; sham-controlled. HBOT CAPS-5 42.57 → 25.8 (p < 0.001), maintained at follow-up. This is the cleanest PTSD RCT to date.
- **Threshold-effect re-analysis 2025** (PMC12373514). Post-hoc analysis: ≥35 % CAPS-5 reduction predicts sustained benefit; below threshold, regression is common.

### Effect sizes and durability

CAPS-5 mean drops of 17–18 points are clinically large (MCID ~10). Durability data extend to 12 months in Doenyas-Barak 2024. Effect appears dose-dependent: 2.0 ATA × 60 sessions outperforms 1.5 ATA × 40 in head-to-head dosage analyses (Harch & Andrews 2024).

### Adjunct vs. standalone positioning

All published authors position HBOT as **adjunct to standard PTSD care** (trauma-focused CBT, EMDR, SSRI). No serious researcher proposes HBOT replacing first-line therapy. Wellcore must echo this: HBOT is not a substitute for trauma-focused psychotherapy.

### Defensible app phrasing

PTSD is a DSM-5 diagnosis. Marketing a wellness product to PTSD sufferers — even with "supports" language — invokes mental-health regulation in both US (FDA, FTC, state psych boards) and TR (Sağlık Bakanlığı reklam kuralları). The July 2025 FDA WHOOP warning letter (Mintz, Arnold & Porter advisories; jmir.org/2026/1/e90882) confirmed that **wellness disclaimers do not protect features whose context implies clinical use**. A goal called "PTSD" with educational content about CAPS-5 reduction is exactly that context. **Recommendation: do not market toward PTSD at all.** Acknowledge in the safety/contraindication FAQ that users in active PTSD treatment should consult their clinician, but no goal card.

## Anxiety Disorders

### Direct evidence

There is **no published RCT** with generalized anxiety disorder, panic disorder, or social anxiety as the primary indication for HBOT. The closest signal is the post-stroke depression literature (e.g., Yan et al. 2025 *Neuropsychiatr Dis Treat*) and some Chinese-language depression trials, none of which are anxiety-primary.

### Secondary-outcome evidence

- Lindell 2025 *Sci Rep*: anxiety subscale of NSI improved more in HBOT arm and persisted at 12 months.
- Doenyas-Barak 2022/2024: anxiety/hyperarousal cluster of CAPS-5 improved alongside the rest.
- Hadanny long-COVID RCT 2024 (*Sci Rep* longitudinal follow-up, doi:10.1038/s41598-024-53091-3): anxiety/depression secondary outcomes improved.

These are encouraging but always in samples already selected for TBI/PTSD/long-COVID. Generalizing to "anxiety in a healthy adult who feels stressed" is unsupported.

### Mechanism speculation

Plausible mechanisms — autonomic rebalancing toward parasympathetic dominance, reduced amygdala reactivity on fMRI in PTSD samples (Doenyas-Barak 2022 imaging), upregulated BDNF and neurogenesis — are biologically reasonable but unproven for primary anxiety disorders.

### Honest assessment for Wellcore

Anxiety as a standalone Wellcore goal is **not defensible** on current evidence. It would require us to either (a) over-claim from secondary outcomes in clinical populations, or (b) invoke DSM-5 anxiety disorders, which triggers regulatory exposure equivalent to PTSD. Anxiety-adjacent benefits (calmer mood, better sleep, reduced rumination) belong inside the existing `wellness` goal as supporting bullets — not a goal.

## Cross-cutting

### Suicide risk screening (recommended Health Screening additions)

TBI roughly doubles, and PTSD roughly triples, lifetime suicide risk vs. general population (multiple epidemiologic studies). One uncontrolled veteran HBOT case series reported reduction in suicidal ideation (10 of 12 with pre-treatment SI no longer endorsed it post-treatment; LSUHSC press release, Harch group). No contradicting data, but no RCT either. This is too thin to claim benefit, but **strong enough to mandate screening**:

**Recommended onboarding addition** (after goal selection, before chamber-fit calibration):

- **EN:** "In the last 2 weeks, have you had thoughts of harming yourself or that you would be better off dead?" — Yes / No / Prefer not to answer
- **TR:** "Son 2 hafta içinde kendinize zarar verme veya yaşamamanın daha iyi olacağı düşüncesi aklınıza geldi mi?" — Evet / Hayır / Cevaplamak istemiyorum

**Trigger logic:**
- "Yes" → immediate non-blocking resource card with TR (Acil Yardım 112; Türkiye Psikiyatri Derneği destek hatları) and EN (988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) numbers, plus "we recommend talking to a clinician before continuing" — user can dismiss but the card persists in Settings → Wellbeing.
- "Prefer not to answer" → same resource card, soft-shown.
- Re-prompt every 90 days for users with `neuro_recovery`, `long_covid`, `brain_fog`, or `wellness` goals.

This is item 9 of the PHQ-9, which has decades of validation. Use that exact wording for liability clarity.

### Validated outcome measures (TR + EN)

Wellcore should adopt **GAD-7** and **PHQ-9** as the weekly mood check, and **modified RPQ** or **NSI** as a monthly check for `neuro_recovery` users. PCL-5 only for users who self-identify as having a trauma history; never auto-shown.

| Instrument | TR validation | EN source | App use |
|---|---|---|---|
| **PHQ-9** (depression, 9 items, 0–27) | Sarı et al. (Turkish reliability, α = 0.84); multiculturalmentalhealth.ca PDF | phqscreeners.com | Weekly mood check; Item 9 = suicidality screen |
| **GAD-7** (anxiety, 7 items, 0–21) | Konkan et al. 2013 (α = 0.85) | hiv.uw.edu/page/mental-health-screening/gad-7 | Weekly anxiety check |
| **PCL-5** (PTSD, 20 items) | Boysan et al. 2017 *Psychiatry Clin Psychopharmacol* 27(3):300-310 (α = 0.94) | ptsd.va.gov | Optional, opt-in only, never default |
| **modified RPQ** (post-concussion, 16 items) | Limited TR validation (use modified RPQ EN with informal translation; flag as not formally validated) | sralab.org/rehabilitation-measures/rivermead | Monthly for `neuro_recovery` |
| **NSI** (neurobehavioral, 22 items) | No published TR validation (use EN; do not deploy to TR users without translation review) | tbi-impact.org/cde | Optional monthly |

**App integration note.** Item-level wording — show the existing validated translations verbatim. Do not paraphrase. Score client-side, store rolling 12-week trend, do not display absolute clinical thresholds (e.g., "you are at moderate risk for depression") — instead show user-facing trends ("your mood scores have improved 4 points over 6 weeks"). Threshold language is exactly the kind of context that triggered the WHOOP letter.

### Veteran/military demographic note

US military/veteran HBOT is a well-organized advocacy ecosystem (TreatNOW, IHA, Brain Injury Coalition). Targeting them invites regulatory scrutiny disproportionate to market size. Turkish veteran (gazi) HBOT scene is small and largely served through state hospitals (GATA/Sağlık Bilimleri Üniversitesi) and a few private centers in İstanbul/Ankara. **Recommendation: do not target veterans as a marketing segment.** Build for the general wellness audience; veterans who self-select and meet contraindication screening can use the product without us courting the segment.

## Wellcore product recommendation

### Goal taxonomy decision

| Condition | Decision | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **TBI / mTBI / PPCS** | **Fold into `neuro_recovery`** | Existing goal already covers post-injury cognitive recovery. Add one bullet referencing "supports recovery from head impacts and concussion-adjacent symptoms" with citation to Lindell 2025. Do not rename the goal. |
| **PTSD** | **Exclude entirely from goal taxonomy** | DSM-5 diagnosis; Doenyas-Barak protocols (2.0 ATA × 60) are clinical-grade and incompatible with Wellcore's home-chamber audience; high regulatory exposure. Acknowledge only in safety/FAQ ("if you are in active treatment for PTSD, consult your clinician before HBOT use"). |
| **Anxiety disorders** | **Fold into `wellness`** | No primary-outcome evidence; secondary-outcome signals do not generalize to primary anxiety. `wellness` already absorbs stress/mood/sleep — add a bullet on "calmer mood and reduced everyday stress." |

### Recommended onboarding flow changes

1. After goal selection, add a **"Health Screening"** step (already planned per Faz 0?) that includes:
   - Existing absolute contraindications (pneumothorax, untreated ear pathology, etc., per `02-contraindications-safety.md`)
   - **New:** PHQ-9 Item 9 suicidality screen (TR + EN wording above)
   - **New (conditional):** if user selected `neuro_recovery`, ask "Have you had a head injury or concussion in the last 12 months?" — if yes, surface a brief educational card noting that HBOT evidence for recent head injury is mixed and they should consult a clinician.
2. Re-screen Item 9 every 90 days; persist resource card.
3. Never auto-show PCL-5; offer it only via Settings → Optional Trackers.
